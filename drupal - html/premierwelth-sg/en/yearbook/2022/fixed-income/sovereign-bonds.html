Fixed Income

KEY HIGHLIGHTS

We prefer credit over sovereign bonds amid a healthy macro backdrop and rising interest rates.

Within Developed Markets, we prefer U.S. HY to U.S. IG on divergent risk-reward.

Within Emerging Markets, we prefer Asia HY in view of the attractive valuation.

Sovereign bonds (Underweight)

Global central banks have embarked on their normalisation path, albeit gradually, as the global economic recovery takes hold. With “substantial further progress” having been made towards maximum employment and price stability goals, the Fed announced at its November Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meeting the beginning of its taper of monthly asset purchases. Meanwhile, the ECB also announced the intent to retire its PEPP come March 2022, while Norway became the first G10 currency country to raise key rates.

No doubt, inflation risks remain skewed to the upside and may lead to a quick pivot towards tighter monetary policy. Having said that, we still believe the pace of policy normalisation will be gradual to avoid derailing economic recovery.

Fed funds futures, a gauge of market expectations, are now pricing in more than two 25 basis point (bps) hikes by end-2022. In contrast, we have a more conservative view and expect one 25 bps hike during the same period. Notably, there are signs of slowing growth after the initial post-COVID-19 surge, and the possibility of the Fed waiting longer than expected to reach pre-COVID-19 employment levels before hiking rates should temper excessive yield rises if any. Hence, we expect the 10-year UST yield to trade within a modestly higher range of 1.5%–2.0% by end-2022.

Overall, we are underweight sovereign bonds from the U.S. and negative-yielding regions like core Europe, as they will likely underperform credit in a rising interest rate environment. In the U.S., from a positioning perspective, we are underweight duration and hence prefer short-to-intermediate-term U.S. government bonds to longer-dated Treasuries.

In contrast, we are positive on China government bonds and expect them to continue to outperform other sovereign bond peers in 2022, underpinned by their relatively attractive absolute yield, as well as increasing acceptance and adoption in global bond indices. Despite a tumultuous year of mainly internal challenges on the regulatory and resources front, the 10-year China government bond traded within a remarkably stable range of less than 50 bps. Although yield differentials have narrowed, in a low interest rate environment, China government bonds continue to offer value with the 10-year bond yielding 2.86% as at end-November 2021 versus the yield on the benchmark 10-year Treasury note at just 1.44% and the 10-year German Bund yield of -0.35%.

Preferred Regions

China

Despite some internal challenges, China’s monetary policy has remained quite controlled. The 10-year China Government Bond (CGB) as at end-November is yielding 3.0%, and is a good choice for investors looking for carry and higher absolute yield.
